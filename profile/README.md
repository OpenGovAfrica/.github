## OpenGov Africa

#### Bridging governments, policy, and technology to drive progress across Africa.

Aligned with the African Union’s Agenda 2063 and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), our mandate focuses on strengthening the pillars of the Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) ecosystem. We prioritize the development of ethical AI frameworks, data sovereignty, and interoperable government systems that ensure no citizen; particularly the most vulnerable, is left behind. Our core programming is governed by a commitment to evidence-based policy and institutional capacity building.
We function as a collaborative platform bridging the gap between citizens, civil society, researchers, and public institutions. At OpenGov Africa, we leverage open data and emerging technologies with a focus on **ethical AI and digital rights** to foster transparency, verify service delivery, and empower informed participation in governance across the continent.

### Our Mission

To catalyze a culture of open, inclusive, and ethical governance in Africa. We provide the tools and data necessary for stakeholders; from the grassroots to the policy level to work together toward improved public outcomes and the protection of vulnerable populations in the digital age.

### Strategic Objectives

* **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI):** Advancing the deployment of resilient, interoperable, and inclusive digital systems that facilitate state-citizen interaction and administrative efficiency.
* **Ethical AI, Digital Rights, and Data Sovereignty:** Establishing governance frameworks for artificial intelligence that prioritizes the protection of vulnerable and marginalized populations, transparency, bias mitigation, and the protection of civil liberties, ensuring African data remains a public asset.
* **Evidence-Based Policy Advocacy:** Utilizing data-driven insights to inform legislative and executive decision-making, ensuring that public policies are responsive to real-time socio-economic needs.
* **Institutional Accountability:** Developing technical tools that enhance the transparency of public expenditure, official career trajectories, and service delivery metrics.

### The Ecosystem

OpenGov Africa is community-driven. We believe that solving Africa’s governance challenges requires a multi-stakeholder approach. Developers,policy researchers, NGO representatives, concerned citizens, are welcome to contribute.

### Technical Governance and Contributions

Our repositories facilitate the development of public interest technology. We maintain strict standards for code quality, documentation, and security to ensure our tools are sustainable and scalable for institutional use.

### Participation
* **Development:** Contributors are encouraged to review our open-source repositories and propose improvements that align with our core technical pillars. Help us build the tools that make government data readable, actionable, and secure.
* **Research & Policy:** We welcome proposals for policy analysis, ethical frameworks, and data modeling.
* **Civil Society & NGO Partners:** Use our platform to amplify your advocacy and share data-backed insights.
* **Citizens:** Track and monitor public projects, and engage in our summits.
