### Security Policy

OpenGov Africa handles sensitive data, including information relating to public officials, government expenditure, and civic activity. We take the security of our platform, repositories, and contributors’ data seriously.

### Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in any OpenGov Africa repository, service, or dataset, please report it privately rather than opening a public issue.

- **Email:** [security@opengovafrica.org](mailto:security@opengovafrica.org)
- **Alternative contact:** [citizens@opengovafrica.org](mailto:citizens@opengovafrica.org) (mark subject line “SECURITY”)
- **Response time:** We aim to acknowledge reports within 5 business days and provide an initial assessment within 14 days.

Please include:

1. A description of the vulnerability and its potential impact.
1. Steps to reproduce the issue.
1. Any relevant logs, screenshots, or proof-of-concept code.
1. Your contact information, if you wish to be credited or updated on resolution.

### Scope

This policy covers:

- OpenGov Africa’s public GitHub repositories and associated infrastructure.
- Data pipelines and tools used to collect, verify, and publish civic data.
- Any web or application interfaces maintained by OpenGov Africa.

This policy does not cover third-party services we link to but do not operate.

### Responsible Disclosure

We ask security researchers to:

- Give us reasonable time to investigate and remediate an issue before public disclosure.
- Avoid accessing, modifying, or deleting data beyond what is necessary to demonstrate a vulnerability.
- Avoid actions that could degrade service availability for other users, including denial-of-service testing.

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith and comply with this policy.

### Data-Specific Sensitivities

Because some datasets reference civil society activity and individuals, a security failure could carry safety consequences for real people, not only data-integrity consequences. Reports involving potential exposure of personal data, including participant information related to civic action datasets, should be flagged as **high priority** in the report subject line.

### Supported Versions

OpenGov Africa is a rolling, community-maintained project. Security fixes are applied to the `main` branch of affected repositories; there are no separate long-term-support branches at this time.

### Acknowledgments

With the reporter’s consent, we will credit valid reports in release notes or a security acknowledgments page.
