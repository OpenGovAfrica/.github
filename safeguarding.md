### Safeguarding and Data Protection Policy

### Purpose

OpenGov Africa collects and publishes information relating to public officials, government expenditure, and civic activity, including protest documentation. Some of this work involves data about identifiable individuals, some of whom may be private citizens, or persons in vulnerable situations. This policy sets out how we handle that responsibility.

This policy applies to all contributors, maintainers, and Steering Committee members, and to every repository and dataset maintained under the OpenGov Africa organization.

-----

### 1. Guiding Principles

- **Purpose limitation:** We collect only the data necessary to fulfill our stated mission of government transparency and accountability. We do not collect data about private individuals for its own sake.
- **Public-interest test:** Before publishing information about any individual, contributors must be able to articulate the public-interest justification — typically, that the individual is a public official acting in an official capacity, or a business entity with a documented conflict of interest.
- **Minimization:** Where a dataset requires information about private citizens (for example, protest documentation), we publish the minimum necessary and avoid incidental disclosure of identity, location, or affiliations not relevant to the record.
- **No data on minors:** OpenGov Africa does not knowingly collect, publish, or retain personal data identifying anyone under the age of 18, except where a minor is a named public office-holder in their own right (which is expected to be rare or non-existent in practice).

### 2. Sensitive Data Categories

The following categories require heightened review before publication:

|Category                                                            |Handling requirement                                                                                                                                |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Protest and civic action participant data                           |Aggregate or anonymize wherever possible. Do not publish names, images, or identifying details of participants without documented, informed consent.|
|Contact details of private individuals                              |Not published unless the individual holds public office or the information is already part of the public record in an official capacity.            |
|Information that could expose a source or contributor to retaliation|Store separately from public datasets; access restricted to designated maintainers.                                                                 |
|Any data relating to a minor                                        |Not collected or published under any circumstances, except as noted in Section 1.                                                                   |

### 3. Verification and Review Before Publication

All data involving named individuals goes through review before merge, consisting of:

1. Confirmation the subject is a public official, public figure acting in an official/public capacity, or an entity with a documented public-interest basis for inclusion.
1. A check for any personal information beyond what is relevant to the public-interest purpose (home address, family details, health information, etc.), which must be removed.
1. For protest/civic action datasets specifically, confirmation that participant-level data has been aggregated, anonymized, or consented to, per Section 2.

## 4. Consent

Where personal data about a private individual is knowingly included in any dataset, we require a documented record of informed consent, including what was disclosed, to whom, and for what purpose. Consent can be withdrawn; see Section 6.

## 5. Reporting a Concern

If you believe any published data:

- Identifies a minor,
- Exposes a private individual without justification or consent,
- Could put a person’s safety at risk, or
- Was collected or published in a way that breaches this policy,

please report it immediately to **[security@opengovafrica.org](mailto:security@opengovafrica.org)**. Reports are treated as urgent and are not made public. We aim to review and, where warranted, take down or redact flagged content within 48 hours of report.

## 6. Right to Removal / Correction

Any individual who believes they are identified in our datasets without adequate public-interest justification, or who wishes to withdraw previously given consent, may request review and removal via the contact above. [e.g., NDPR (Nigeria), POPIA (South Africa), or GDPR where relevant to data subjects.]

## 7. Contributor Responsibilities

All contributors handling personal data are expected to:

- Read and follow this policy and the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- Escalate uncertain cases to the Data Review Sub-Team rather than publishing unilaterally.
- Never share sensitive or unpublished datasets outside approved project channels.

## 8. Policy Ownership and Review

This policy is maintained by the Steering Committee (see [governance.md](./governance.md)) and is reviewed at least annually, or immediately following any reported incident.
