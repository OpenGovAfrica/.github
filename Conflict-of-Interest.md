### Conflict of Interest Policy

### Purpose

OpenGov Africa’s credibility depends on the independence and impartiality of its data, analysis, and decision-making. This policy sets out how actual, potential, and perceived conflicts of interest are identified, disclosed, and managed across the organization, and complements the governance structure described in [governance.md](./governance.md).

### Who This Applies To

- All Steering Committee members
- Core Maintainers and Team Leads
- Any contributor engaged in data verification, publication decisions, or fund allocation
- Donor and partner representatives holding Advisory, Observer, or Voting seats, as defined in governance.md

### What Counts as a Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when a person’s personal, financial, political, or organizational interests could reasonably be seen to influence their judgment or actions within OpenGov Africa. This includes, but is not limited to:

- Financial relationships with a government official, ministry, agency, or business entity that is the subject of OpenGov Africa data or reporting.
- Political party membership, candidacy, or active campaign involvement, where this relates to officials or entities covered by our datasets.
- Family or close personal relationships with a public official or entity under review.
- A funder or partner organization whose funding is tied, explicitly or implicitly, to a favorable or unfavorable treatment of specific data, findings, or public officials.
- Personal or organizational financial benefit from decisions about fund allocation, vendor selection, or donor governance seats.

### Disclosure Requirements

- Individuals in the roles listed above must disclose any known conflict of interest as soon as it arises, and no later than before participating in any related decision, review, or publication.
- Disclosures are made to the Steering Committee and logged in the organization’s public governance records, consistent with the transparency commitments in governance.md.
- Disclosure is required even where the individual believes the conflict would not affect their judgment. The obligation is to disclose, not to self-assess materiality.

### Management of Conflicts

Once disclosed, a conflict is managed as follows:

1. **Recusal:** The individual does not participate in the discussion, review, verification, or vote relating to the matter in question.
1. **Documentation:** The recusal and the nature of the conflict (without necessarily disclosing private financial details) are recorded in public meeting notes or governance logs.
1. **Independent review:** Where a conflict affects a specific dataset or publication decision, a second, uninvolved reviewer confirms the final output.

### Donor and Partner-Specific Safeguards

Consistent with Section 4 of [governance.md](./governance.md):

- Donor or partner representatives with Steering Committee seats may not vote on matters directly tied to their own funding, organization, or affiliated interests.
- No funding relationship may be conditioned, formally or informally, on favorable treatment of specific officials, institutions, or datasets. Any such attempt must be disclosed and is grounds for review of the funding relationship.

### Failure to Disclose

Failure to disclose a known conflict of interest is treated as a breach of the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and may result in removal from a role or the Steering Committee, per the recall process described in governance.md.

### Reporting a Suspected Undisclosed Conflict

Concerns about an undisclosed conflict of interest can be raised confidentially at **[citizens@opengovafrica.org](mailto:citizens@opengovafrica.org)**, marked “CONFLICT OF INTEREST,” or raised with the Steering Committee directly.

### Policy Ownership and Review

This policy is maintained by the Steering Committee and reviewed at least annually.
